# Trinidad Chambliss

*Ole Miss, by way of Ferris State. Early 2027 class profile, written in the summer of 2026. Nobody has declared for anything.*

Chambliss almost never gives the ball away, and he does it while going after throws most quarterbacks his size would leave alone. Aggression without the turnovers is a rare pairing, and it carried a Division II transfer into the SEC and onto real draft radars. The catch is the body it comes in.

## Where the evaluations agree

The feel is the product. He plays with natural pocket presence and the kind of poise that shows up most right after a mistake, when he answers with a good rep instead of a worse one. He layers touch and anticipation onto hard throws, and the ball security is not a gift of a soft schedule: he kept the interceptions near zero even against ranked opponents, which is where a gambler's aggression usually catches up to him. He is a genuine mover too, pass-first, buying time to throw before he thinks about running.

## The open questions

The body sets the ceiling. He sits at the low end of the position's size, and the release runs long enough that the ball is exposed to hands at the line, so a batted pass is a live risk on a timing throw. Neither is small at the next level, where the edges are longer and the windows are tighter.

The mechanics leave a specific tell. His short-area placement can be worse than his intermediate and deep placement, which is backwards from the usual curve and awkward for an offense that lives on rhythm throws underneath. And the same aggression that produces the difficult completions will sometimes talk him into a forced throw or past an open checkdown.

## Where the evaluations split

His instincts are not in question, and neither is his size. No amount of film is going to change either one. What nobody can tell you yet is whether the first survives the second, and a summer is not going to answer it.

## What I'm watching in 2026

Whether he can shorten the release without draining the touch out of it, because that motion is doing double duty as a strength and a liability. Whether the short-area placement climbs to meet the rest of his game. And whether the poise under pressure holds across a full season rather than a stretch, since that feel is the load-bearing trait in his case.
