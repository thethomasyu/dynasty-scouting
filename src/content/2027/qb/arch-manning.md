# Arch Manning

*Texas. Early 2027 class profile, written in the summer of 2026, before his second year as the starter. Nobody has declared for anything.*

When the pocket starts to give, Manning climbs into the space the rush leaves instead of backing off his platform, and he resets his feet with bodies around them. He'll get the ball out of a muddy pocket, off balance when he has to be. The sacks stay low.

## Where the evaluations agree

The deep ball comes down with touch. He layers it over one defender and in front of the help, and he drops his arm slot to fit the window instead of forcing every throw through the same release. Off-platform, the ball still arrives where he wants it.

He gets past the first read with his eyes and feet still connected, and he throws with anticipation, releasing before a receiver has come open.

## The open questions

The short stuff is where it wanders. When he rushes his feet and skips the mechanics the easy throws come out loose, dropped at a receiver's feet or put on the wrong shoulder, the layups a top passer banks without thinking. The misses cluster on the throws that ask the least of him, and they trace to footwork more than to arm or to a read he can't make.

Interior pressure still gets to him. The movement handles edge rush and general muck, but a rusher up the middle can knock his timing off schedule. The cleaner late-season tape sits next to a choppier early stretch it doesn't cancel out.

## Where the evaluations split

On the arm, the touch and the release flexibility are settled; the drive velocity isn't. The motion is compact and a little sidearm, and how much that takes off the throws living on pure velocity, the deep out, the dig into a tightening window, is the unsettled part. Against ranked opponents his completion rate held while his yards per attempt came down, which is the same question showing up in the box score.

## What I'm watching in 2026

The one to watch is the short-area placement, and whether it steadies without costing him the compact release.
