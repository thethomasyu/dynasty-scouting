# Dante Moore

*Oregon, by way of UCLA. Early 2027 class profile, written in the summer of 2026. He has confirmed a return for the 2026 season, so nothing about the draft is assumed.*

The release comes out clean and on time, and inside structure Moore rarely needs more than that. The base stays under him, the accuracy holds at every level, and he'll read a defender's leverage to take the shortcut instead of grinding through the whole progression.

What is not settled is how he plays once the pocket stops cooperating.

## Where the evaluations agree

The accuracy is the least arguable part of his game. He places the ball rather than just completing it, drops touch into the levels that ask for it, and keeps rhythm concepts on time so the throw arrives before the window moves. The processing travels with it: he confirms or discards a read off one defender's movement instead of scanning the whole field, and the accuracy stays intact on movement throws as long as the play stays near its design.

## The open questions

Pressure is the crack. When it comes, the clean process can come apart: the eyes drop and the downfield throw that was open goes unthrown. The drift shows up most in the higher-leverage games, which are the ones a projection actually cares about. The box score leaves a fingerprint in the same place, with his interceptions bunched against ranked opponents and in second halves, when the pocket gets harder and the game speeds up. That does not prove the cause, but the film worry and the numbers point at the same spot.

Underneath the pressure question is a quieter physical one. The drive power reads as no better than adequate and the frame draws flags, and that pairing is why some see a lower ceiling than the top of the class. None of it touches the accuracy. It sets a limit on how much the accuracy can be asked to carry.

## Where the evaluations split

Everything about Moore is settled except the pressure response, and that one trait is carrying the whole disagreement. What the current tape can't tell you is whether the drift is a young starter still learning to hold his platform, or the arm and the frame quietly setting a limit on how far the accuracy travels. A clean season doesn't produce enough of the hard reps to know, and I'm not going to pretend it does.

## What I'm watching in 2026

Whether his eyes stay downfield after the first hint of heat instead of dropping into a check-and-drift, and whether he still attacks a tight window once the called plan has broken. The frame question runs underneath both: he needs to add play strength without pulling the clean mechanics out of tune.
