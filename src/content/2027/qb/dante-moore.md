# Dante Moore

*Oregon, by way of UCLA. Early 2027 class profile, written in the summer of 2026. He has confirmed a return for the 2026 season, so nothing about the draft is assumed.*

Moore has maybe the cleanest mechanical foundation in this group, and inside structure he rarely needs more than it. The release comes out on time and repeatable, the base stays under him, the accuracy holds at every level, and he's sharp enough to read a defender's leverage and take the shortcut instead of grinding through the whole progression. It's easy, professional-looking quarterbacking. Then the pocket stops cooperating, and the entire evaluation slides to what happens next. That isn't a knock so much as a map of where to look. Almost everything about him is settled, and the one unsettled thing happens to be the one that decides how high he goes.

## Where the evaluations agree

The accuracy is the least arguable thing on the tape. He places the ball, he doesn't just complete it, and there's a real difference between those. Completing it is getting it to the guy. Placing it is putting it where the catch becomes more yards, on the front shoulder away from the leverage, into the exact level the concept was drawn for. He drops touch where a throw asks for touch and keeps rhythm concepts on time, so the ball shows up before the window slides. The processing rides along with it. He'll confirm or kill a read off one defender's movement rather than scanning the whole field, and the accuracy stays intact on the move as long as the play is living somewhere near its design.

## The open questions

Pressure is the crack, and it's worth being precise about what actually cracks. When the heat comes, the clean process can come apart from the eyes down. They drop off the coverage and onto the rush, and the downfield throw that was open goes unthrown while he's busy feeling the pocket. That drift shows up most in the higher-leverage games, which are, inconveniently, the exact games a projection cares about. The box score leaves a fingerprint in the same place: his interceptions bunch against ranked opponents and in second halves, when the pocket tightens and the game speeds up on him. A stat can't prove a cause on its own. But when the film worry and the number are pointing at the same spot, you stop filing it under coincidence.

Sitting under the loud question is a quieter, more physical one. The drive power reads no better than adequate, the frame draws flags, and that pairing is why some evaluators see a lower ceiling here than at the very top of the class. None of it touches the accuracy. What it does is set a limit on how much the accuracy can be asked to carry by itself.

## Where the evaluations split

The split is really one question in a trenchcoat. Everything about Moore is agreed on except the pressure response, and that single trait is carrying the whole disagreement. What the current tape genuinely cannot tell you is which player you're watching: a young starter who hasn't yet learned to hold his platform when it gets loud, or the arm and the frame quietly drawing a line on how far the accuracy travels. Those are two different careers, and a clean, winning season is precisely the season least likely to produce the hard reps that would tell them apart. I'm not going to pretend it does. The evidence that settles this mostly hasn't been created yet.

## What I'm watching in 2026

Whether the eyes stay up and downfield after the first hint of heat instead of bailing into a check-and-drift. Whether he'll still put a throw into a tight window once the called plan has fallen apart and nobody's schemed anyone open. And the frame runs beneath both: he has to add real play strength without knocking the clean mechanics out of tune, which is a trickier ask than it sounds, because the easy way to get stronger is to get thicker and slower, and slow is the one thing this game can't spare.
