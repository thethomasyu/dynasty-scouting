# LaNorris Sellers

*South Carolina. Early 2027 class profile, written in the summer of 2026. Nobody has declared for anything.*

A lot of Sellers's best plays start as bad ones. When the protection caves, the legs answer it, and he slips the rush for yards a broken pocket shouldn't give up. When the trouble is his own, a beat too long on the trigger, the arm covers for it and fits the ball in after the window has started to close. The talent keeps bailing out unstable snaps, some the offense handed him and some he made himself.

## Where the evaluations agree

The physical tools aren't disputed. The arm is rare and the deep ball that comes off it is a real weapon. The legs threaten a defense on designed keepers and on scrambles, and he's strong enough to take a hit and keep the play alive.

## The open questions

The trouble is timing. He'll sit on a read until the receiver is clearly open instead of throwing it on the route's schedule, and that hitch is expensive against a rush that's already coming. The short, on-rhythm touch throw, the five-yard ball that keeps an offense on schedule, isn't dependable yet.

The sacks come in volume, and a chunk of them trace to the same delay, holding for a certainty that shows up late. The outlet doesn't reliably save him either: his access to the checkdown that would turn a sack into a short gain comes and goes.

Under pressure he can turn jumpy before he turns dangerous, though he'll shrug off a tackler once one actually reaches him. The third-down line is the ugliest stretch of his season, under half his throws completed and half of his interceptions on the down where the answer has to arrive on time.

## Where the evaluations split

Some of it the structure made harder: a line that didn't hold, and a rigid pro-style scheme that gave him few quick, built-in answers, the outlets and easy completions that let a young quarterback play fast. The late clock and the checkdowns he leaves sitting are on him.

## What I'm watching in 2026

The tell is the trigger. Does he start throwing before the receiver has clearly won, or does the same late release show up behind better protection. A quicker scheme can hand him easier reads; it can't get the ball out of his hand for him.
