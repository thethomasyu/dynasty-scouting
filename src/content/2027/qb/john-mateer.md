# John Mateer

*Oklahoma, by way of Washington State. Early 2027 class profile, written in the summer of 2026. Oklahoma confirmed right-hand surgery after the September Auburn game, so the 2025 tape splits into a healthy stretch and a compromised one. Nobody has declared for anything.*

Mateer plays quarterback at a sprint. Quick feet, a fast release, arm angles he makes up on the move, and a runner's first instinct to take off the moment the initial read is not open. It generates highlights and it generates giveaways in about equal measure, and the hand surgery in the middle of the season makes it harder to know how much of the bad belongs to him.

## Where the evaluations agree

The playmaking is the draw, and it is genuine. The feet are quick, the release is fast, and he escapes trouble and creates as a runner in a way the pocket passers on this list cannot. When the structure breaks he throws from platforms and angles that should not work and sometimes do, and the big plays are real rather than manufactured. There is a competitive fearlessness to how he plays that carries onto the tape.

## The open questions

The arm and the decisions are where it gets hard. His arm is adequate at best for the next level, and he compensates by driving everything, so the soft, layered throw that a checkdown or a touch window wants is not in his bag yet. The bigger issue is judgment. The aggression turns into turnover-worthy throws, and his answer to early trouble is too often to run rather than reset in the pocket and find the second option. A quarterback who bails to his legs at the first sign of a covered read gives up throws that were there for the taking.

## Where the evaluations split

Almost everything about the projection runs through the hand. He had surgery on his throwing hand in the middle of the season, and the worst of the decision-making clustered right around it. That timing is the problem. It means last year's film can't separate a compromised hand from a compromised process, and those two carry completely different futures. A healthy season is the only thing that untangles it, and it has not happened yet.

## What I'm watching in 2026

Whether a changeup shows up, some sign he can take something off a throw instead of drilling every one. Whether a healthy hand quiets the whole-body mechanics the compensation seems to feed. And whether the playmaking can happen inside structure, on schedule, without sanding off the part that makes him dangerous.
