# Julian Sayin

*Ohio State, by way of Alabama. Early 2027 class profile, written in the summer of 2026. Nobody has declared for anything.*

There is no arguing with how accurate Sayin was last year. The argument is about what produced it, a genuinely quick and anticipatory passer or one of the best supporting casts in the country doing part of the work for him. Both are on the tape, and they are hard to pull apart.

## Where the evaluations agree

The accuracy comes first, and it is real. He places the clean-pocket ball and throws the seam with anticipation, and the operation stays on time. He gets more on the ball than his build suggests by driving with his whole body, a useful trick even when it takes visible effort. Once he is past his first read, he mostly stays out of trouble with it.

## The open questions

The margins are thin everywhere. He is a smaller quarterback with little room to absorb a hit or a bad platform, and the arm is adequate rather than big, so the deep ball and the drive throw take his whole body to make. The concern that matters most is life after the first read: when the first answer is covered, the progression slows and the sacks come, and a lot of his best tape arrives with that first window open on schedule. The box score leaves a trace of it too. Against ranked opponents the completion rate slid out of the seventies and the giveaways climbed, the shape you would see if the margin his supporting cast provides is doing real work. Ohio State keeps handing him that first window. An NFL defense will not.

## Where the evaluations split

A completion number is a fact. What produced it is the argument, and the raw data cannot hand you the answer. Strip the protection and the early separation his receivers won, and either the accuracy still stands on its own or it was leaning on them the whole time. Nobody gets to know which until he plays somewhere the margin is thinner.

## What I'm watching in 2026

Whether he can keep the offense on schedule once the first read is taken away. Whether he will drive the middle of the field under pressure instead of drifting off it. And how much of the production is still there when his receivers stop winning early for him, which a full season without the current safety net starts to answer.
