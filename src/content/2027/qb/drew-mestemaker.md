# Drew Mestemaker

*Oklahoma State, by way of North Texas. Early 2027 class profile, written in the summer of 2026. Nobody has declared for anything, and 2028 is a live possibility.*

Two seasons ago, Mestemaker was a punter and a safety who backed up at quarterback. Last fall he started a full year at North Texas and played like none of that was true: real velocity, a deep ball that comes out with shape, and progression reads, full-field and half-field, that a player this new to the position has no business already owning.

That gap, between how little he has played quarterback and how much he already does well, is what makes him worth studying and what makes him almost impossible to place.

## Where the evaluations agree

The arm is the easy part. The velocity is natural and the deep ball comes out with shape, and he throws to all three levels without hesitating. The reads are the harder thing to explain. He works full-field and half-field progressions, identifies pressure and gets the protection or the play changed, and keeps his eyes downfield on the later options instead of dumping the ball the moment the first look closes. For a quarterback who was punting two years ago, that part is not supposed to be there yet.

## The open questions

The body comes first, because a lot of the shaky moments trace back to it. He needs mass and functional strength, and without them the drive throws lose steam and the deep ball that leaves clean will sometimes flutter or tail late. The lower half is the tell. While the first read is there, the base holds; the moment he has to come off it, the feet get choppy and the mechanics loosen, and the ball starts to sail or die.

The other thing is that he can decide too early. Predetermined throws and quick checkdowns show up, and they cut concepts off before they develop, so the offense settles for the safe completion underneath instead of the bigger one the design was about to spring. On a young quarterback that can be nerves or it can be habit, and this sample cannot tell you which.

Underneath all of it is the setting. One season, at a Group of Five program, from a player who had barely started before. That is a real beginning and a thin case at the same time, and the level he beat is exactly what a year at this one, against real defenses, has to re-prove.

## Where the evaluations split

None of the disagreement is about the film. Everyone sees the arm and the reads that should not be there yet. What is in dispute is trust: how much a single thin season, at a soft level, from a player this new to the job, is allowed to prove. It is a patience question more than a talent one, and it lands on the calendar, on whether the honest read of him is a 2027 quarterback or a 2028 one. A summer of practice reports will not settle that. Another season against real defenses might.

## What I'm watching in 2026

Whether added strength turns into more on the drive throw and steadier play late in games. Whether the feet and the mechanics stay connected when he is forced off the first read, since that is where the whole thing currently wobbles. And how he looks against defenses that can actually challenge him, because that is the exact test last year did not give him. He may not be a 2027 prospect at all; if this season is really an audition for 2028, the useful thing it can show is whether the holes are closing.
