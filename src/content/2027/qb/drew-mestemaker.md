# Drew Mestemaker

*Oklahoma State, by way of North Texas. Early 2027 class profile, written in the summer of 2026. Nobody has declared for anything, and 2028 is a live possibility.*

Two seasons ago, Mestemaker was a punter and a safety who happened to back up at quarterback. Last fall he started a full year at North Texas and played like none of that was in his file: real velocity, a deep ball that comes out with actual shape on it, and progression reads, full-field and half-field both, that a player this new to the position has no business already owning.

That gap is the whole story. On one side, how little he has actually played quarterback. On the other, how much he already does well. It's what makes him fascinating and what makes him close to impossible to place, because the usual tools you'd use to project a passer all quietly assume he's been one for a while.

## Where the evaluations agree

The arm is the easy part to describe. The velocity is natural, the deep ball leaves with shape, and he attacks all three levels without a hitch of hesitation. The reads are the harder part to explain, and the more interesting one. He works full-field and half-field progressions, he identifies pressure and gets the protection slid or the play changed, and he keeps his eyes downfield on the later options instead of bailing to the checkdown the second his first look closes. Sit with what that means in context. A guy who was punting two years ago is doing the between-the-ears work that usually takes quarterbacks four or five years to trust. It isn't supposed to be there yet. It's there.

## The open questions

The body comes first, because a lot of the shaky moments trace straight back to it. He needs mass and functional strength, and without them the drive throws lose steam and the deep ball that left his hand clean will sometimes flutter or tail off late. The lower half is the tell, and a revealing one. While the first read is open, the base holds under him and everything looks pro. The moment he has to come off that read, the feet go choppy, the mechanics loosen, and the ball starts to sail or die. His mechanics are still read-dependent, in other words. They hold while the picture is simple and come undone when it isn't, which is a developmental signature more than a talent flaw.

The other thing is that he can decide too early. Predetermined throws show up, and quick checkdowns, and both of them cut a concept off before it develops, so the offense takes the safe five yards underneath instead of the bigger thing the design was a beat from springing. On a quarterback this raw that can be nerves or it can be a habit, and this sample can't tell you which. Underneath all of it sits the setting: one season, at a Group of Five program, from a player who'd barely taken a college snap before it. That's a real beginning and a thin case at the same time, and the level of defense he carved up is exactly what a year at a Big 12 program, against real speed, has to re-prove.

## Where the evaluations split

None of the disagreement is actually about the film. Everyone sees the arm, and everyone sees the reads that shouldn't be there yet. What's in dispute is trust, meaning how much a single thin season, at a soft level, from a player this new to the job, is allowed to prove about the next one. That isn't a talent argument. It's a question of how much rope you hand a tiny sample, and it lands right on the calendar. The honest read of him might be a 2027 quarterback and it might be a 2028 one, and a summer of glowing practice notes won't budge that an inch. Another full season against defenses that can actually challenge him could budge it a lot.

## What I'm watching in 2026

Whether the added strength turns into more on the drive throw and steadier play in the fourth quarter. Whether the feet and the mechanics stay connected once he's forced off the first read, since that's the exact seam where the whole thing currently comes apart. And how he holds up against defenses with the talent to make him uncomfortable, because that's the test last season never once gave him. He may not be a 2027 prospect at all. If this year turns out to be an audition for 2028, the most useful thing it can do is show whether the holes are closing or staying put.
