# Reed Harris

*Arizona State, by way of Boston College. Early 2027 class profile, written in the summer of 2026, before his senior season. A sleeper file: one good look, plenty still dark. Nobody has declared for anything yet.*

Reed Harris is the kind of prospect scouting departments file under "go check": a spring-verified 6'4" and 220 pounds with speed informally pegged in the mid-to-high 4.4s. Treat that speed figure as the estimate it is, and note what it would mean if it's close: receivers at that size with that gear are scarce, and this one spent his college career hidden in plain sight, catching 39 balls for 673 yards in one of the country's worst passing situations.

What the tape shows is a radius game and a power game. Harris uses his full catch radius the way big receivers are supposed to and often don't: real vertical leaping on jump balls, long arms, big hands, and repeated unusual adjustments to badly placed throws, the skill his quarterback situation forced him to practice constantly. The routes are the cost side: he wins through mass and momentum rather than craft, muscling through coverage where refined receivers slip through it, and the silkiness and consistency aren't there yet. His development question is a fair one to ask out loud: how do you refine a player who has always won by being bigger and faster than the problem?

One indirect clue on his file is worth more than it looks: Boston College used him extensively as an H-back and tight-end-style blocker. Coaching staffs don't hand that job to soft receivers. The usage suppressed his receiving résumé and doubles as testimony about his toughness, and the path explains the rawness too: he's a former Montana high school quarterback, which means he's been a full-time receiver for less time than most of this class.

The rest is dark. Press answers, after-catch value, spatial feel, real speed, real contested numbers: all unexamined or estimated, and the contested results that do exist ran behind what his usage demanded, with terrible quarterback play as part of the story. Now he lands in a dramatically better offense at Arizona State, opposite Omarion Miller, with a real chance at the featured-role season his profile has never had.

This is a sketch, then, not a portrait. The projection if it fills in kindly: a boundary size-speed receiver whose toughness is pre-certified and whose polish arrives late. Watch whether the routes gain any craft beyond collision, whether the contested numbers climb with a functional quarterback, and whether the speed estimate survives being measured. A breakout wouldn't surprise anyone who's seen the build. Neither would another year of mass-and-momentum football, and the difference between those outcomes is most of his draft value.
