# Jordan Faison

*Notre Dame. Early 2027 class profile, written in the summer of 2026, entering his first season as a one-sport athlete. Nobody has declared for anything yet.*

The argument for Jordan Faison is a slope, not a level. Two years ago he was a national-champion lacrosse player, a two-star football afterthought who started every game for the title team in his other sport and moonlighted as a receiver. Last season, the moonlighting stopped looking like moonlighting: the routes went from an athlete's routes to a route runner's, with detail, nuance, and intention arriving where raw movement used to be. The hands jumped with them, down to a sub-four-percent drop rate. Development curves that steep are rare, and they're the entire case here, because now football gets all of him for the first time.

The skill that's already bankable is after the catch. Faison forced 19 missed tackles on just 49 receptions last season, an outlier rate on modest volume, built on quick-twitch burst, real shiftiness, and an instant catcher-to-runner conversion he's now shown two years running. He beats the first man like it's a habit. Pair that with real spatial awareness, a knack for finding the soft spots in zone, and the 640-yard season underneath the rates starts reading like a floor instead of a ceiling.

The structural facts point where you'd guess. At 5'11" and 185, the projection is a free-release slot; the press question is a projection rather than an observed failure, since nobody has really pressed him yet, but nothing about the frame volunteers for that fight. The one blemish on the toughness-adjacent ledger is ball security in the return game, three career muffed punts, which complicates the special-teams value his athleticism otherwise advertises.

So the projection: a slot with real after-catch juice and a development arrow pointing sharply up, in an offense that should feed exactly that. The bet is the slope. If the 2024-to-2025 improvement continues now that lacrosse is behind him, the current evaluation is too low and this file gets happily rewritten. If the curve flattens, he's a useful rotational slot with a great story. I'm watching whether the route detail deepens again, what happens the first time someone jams him, and whether the muffs stop. Two straight years of steep improvement earn a player the benefit of the doubt on a third.
